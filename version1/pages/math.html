About Math

The first math textbook I read was Book of Proof by Richard Hammack. It introduced me to fundamental concepts in discrete mathematics, such as logic and set theory, and laid the foundation for my mathematical journey.

What I find most fascinating about math is how seemingly unrelated ideas often reveal deep connections or turn out to be part of a broader framework. For instance, the conditions under which a matrix has a left or right inverse are essentially the same as those for functions in general. Similarly, the Rank-Nullity Theorem in Linear Algebra is actually a special case of the First Isomorphism Theorem in Group Theory. More recently, I discovered both Stokes' Theorem and Divergence Theorem could be thought of as generalisations of Green's Theorem. The former of which is well-known; the latter, not so much, as evidenced by the number of confused comments from people seeing Green's Theorem (Normal Form) for the first time.
