MA1522 Linear Algebra for Computing

Taken in 24/25 Sem 1
Lecturers: Jonathan Teo

  1. Linear Systems
  2. Real Vector Space
  3. Orthogonality
  4. Eigenvectors
  5. Linear Transformations

MA1522 follows MIT's Linear Algebra by Gilbert Strang closely, with concepts like LU factorisation, QR factorisation and Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), which are not included in the math-major variant of MA2001. In addition, MATLAB is actively used in this module â€” in exams too.

The introductory linear algebra course was definitely the most intiminating math course, partly because we are used to doing Calculus since 15 in Singapore. Linear Algebra is an entirely different field. Although we learnt tangential concepts like normal vectors for A-Level, the presentation is very different. This field is a lot more theoretical than most low-level math modules, as there are lots of definitions and theorems (with proofs).

I struggled with the Midterm here, as I did not have a great conceptual understanding then, and I was not very good at using MATLAB. I did manage to do quite well for finals fortunately, and got an A for this course. Overall, this is my one of my favourite modules in the first semester of university. Seeing everything come together at the end with the introduction of Linear Transformations was also greatly satisfying. This truly was one of the first pure math mods in university, despite its heavy computational nature. Very excited to delve into Linear Algebra II.
