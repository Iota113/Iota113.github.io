CS2040S Data Structures and Algorithms

Taken in 24/25 Sem 2
Lecturers: Eldon Chung and Diptarka Chakraborty

  1. Searching and Sorting Algorithms
  2. Trees
  3. Hashing
  4. Heaps and Union-Find
  5. Graphs, Single Source Shortest Paths, Minimum Spanning Trees
  6. Dynamic Programming

Data Structures and Algorithms was a greatly enjoyable course for a math fan like me. In addition to the cool ideas in general, there are also some concepts tangential to pure math, e.g. the shortest distance on a graph forming a metric space, the idea of using graphs as a state space, similar to phase diagrams from Physics and Markov Processes from Probability. In a more explicit case, Dynamic Programming (DP) is really just a form of mathematical optimisation. Apparently, the person who pioneered DP — Bellman (same person from Bellman-Ford) — named it so instead of something more mathematical in fear of his research being rejected, his superior then was greatly against anything math or theory.

The course itself was highly theoretical, with very little emphasis placed on implemeting the ideas in code. Most of the work is presented in pseudo-code. And sometimes, even that is excessive as giving high-level ideas is usually sufficient. As such, CS2040S is not a replacement for grinding leetcode. However, I feel I have a strong conceptual understanding of most DSA concepts by the end; all that remains is familiarising myself with Java and Python syntax.

Overall, one of my favourites this semester. I am now more convinced I would like to delve into Algorithms for my CS focus area.
