Some History

The "first math textbook" I read is Book of Proof by Richard Hammack. It introduced me to basic discrete math concepts such as logic and sets, and paved the way for my future math education.

I think what I like the most about math is how seemingly disconnected concepts are related, or together part of a bigger idea. For example, the conditions for matrices having left and right inverses are actually just the conditions for functions having those in general, or how the Rank-Nullity Theorem from Linear Algebra is a special case of the First Isomorphism Theorem from Group Theory??? Something I still don't understand. Generalisation is the name of the game, and I think it is through the pursuit for that that leads to true understanding. Now I don't consider myself all that great at math, but I enjoy the subject and would like to document some of my favourite moments learning it.
