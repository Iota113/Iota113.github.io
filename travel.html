Travel

Travelling is fun! If I win a lottery and never have to worry about money again, I would probably spend all my time travelling the world. Unfortunately, I have yet to win a lottery, so here's a very limited view of that world. Click on the photos below to get to each country's specific section.

China

China's greatest gift is its immense land mass; there is nothing quite like witnessing the massive mountains and valleys of places like Zhangjiajie and Wulong, Chongqing. Cities like Xi'An and Hangzhou, on other other hand, offer a sneak peek into this land's history, spanning over thosuands of years. Another standout aspect is China's architecture; modern hubs like Sanlitun from Beijing—one of my favourite shopping districts—showcase its rapid urban development. However, this modernisation also comes at a cost. As more areas become commercialized, they risk losing the sense of immersion that makes them special. Thus, one needs to dig deeper for a more authentic expereience. Then again, I suppose that is true for every place to some extent.

Japan

My favourite aspect of Japan is its convenience. Getting around is convenient thanks to a highly efficient public transportation system, well-integrated with tools like Google Maps. Communication is conveninent as Japan is generally tourist-friendly and many service staff carry a translation device. Convenience store is, well, convenient. Obviously, convenience alone does not make a destination worthwhile. In Japan's case, its ease of travel is paired with immense beauty and a rich cultural heritage, making it a prime location to visit.
